Project Overview
This project explores the end-to-end digital manufacturing of 3D printed phone casings for the fictional client Phones-R-Us. The project integrates additive manufacturing, FESTO automation systems, Industry 4.0 principles, digital traceability, and Tecnomatix Plant Simulation to achieve an annual production target of 50,000 units.

Engineering Journey

Week 1 — PrintCity Induction

The journey began with the mandatory additive manufacturing induction at PrintCity led by Gary Buller. The induction covered machine operation, material handling, workflow procedures, and industrial printing practices before producing the very first black PLA bottom case.

Week 2 — CAD Design Development

The bottom case was developed using the provided tutorial, while the top case required independent interpretation of engineering drawings in Fusion 360. Early versions intentionally excluded snap-fit geometry to validate the primary form first.

Weeks 3–5 — Parameter Optimisation

Approximately 50 print iterations were produced using Ultimaker S3, Ultimaker S5, and Bambu Lab P1S printers. Parameters such as infill, speed, supports, skin layers, brim width, and snap-fit tolerances were systematically optimised.

Weeks 6–7 — QR Traceability System

A dual extrusion QR code system was integrated into the top case using white PLA for the body and black PLA for QR code and supports. Each QR code linked directly to a Digital Part Record PDF, implementing a component-level digital twin.

Week 8 — FESTO CP Lab Testing

Final iterations were tested on the FESTO CP Lab assembly line. Multiple cases successfully passed the Measuring and Muscle Press stations, while others required further tolerance refinement due to tight or loose snap-fit conditions.

Week 9 — Final Recovery

One week before submission, all physical iterations were accidentally lost during travel. With only two days remaining, the final successful versions were rebuilt entirely from accumulated manufacturing knowledge, producing parts that passed both FESTO validation stages.

Additive Manufacturing Journey
Different parameter combinations were explored to improve surface finish, dimensional accuracy, snap-fit behaviour, and QR code readability. The project highlighted the inconsistency challenges of FDM manufacturing and the importance of iterative engineering refinement.

FESTO CP Lab Validation
The FESTO CP Lab assembly line consisted of six integrated stations: Magazine, Measuring, Pick-by-Light, Muscle Press, Tunnel Furnace, and Workpiece Discharge. The critical validation stages were the Measuring station and Muscle Press station, where dimensional accuracy and snap-fit functionality were evaluated.

QR Traceability & Digital Twin
Each phone case included an embedded QR code printed using dual extrusion technology. Scanning the QR code opened a Digital Part Record PDF containing manufacturing data, printer information, parameters, inspection status, and traceability information.

Tecnomatix Plant Simulation
Tecnomatix Plant Simulation was used to analyse production throughput, bottlenecks, worker utilisation, and annual production capability. Simulation results showed that 3D printing was the system bottleneck, while increasing workers had no effect on throughput.
