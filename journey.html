Engineering Journey

Week 1

PrintCity Induction & First Print

The project began with mandatory printer induction at PrintCity. The session introduced machine operation, material handling, QR scan-before-printing workflow and additive manufacturing practices.

The first bottom case was printed in black PLA. Although the geometry was acceptable, the dark material made dimensional inspection difficult.

  • Initial dimensional validation
  • Black PLA inspection issue
  • Beginning of iterative workflow
Figure 1 — First black PLA bottom case printed during Week 1.
Week 2

Fusion 360 CAD Development

The bottom case was designed using the instructor-provided workflow. The top case required interpreting engineering drawings independently and reconstructing the geometry directly inside Fusion 360.

Initial versions intentionally excluded the snap-fit mechanism to validate the primary geometry before introducing tolerance-sensitive features.

Figure 2 — Fusion 360 CAD modelling and geometry development.
Weeks 3–5

Parameter Optimisation & Print Iterations

More than 50 print iterations were produced across Ultimaker S3, Ultimaker S5 and Bambu Lab P1S printers.

Parameters including infill density, layer resolution, print speed and support structures were continuously adjusted to improve dimensional consistency and assembly quality.

Figure 3 — Ultimaker S5 production setup at PrintCity.
Weeks 6–7

QR Code Traceability & Digital Twin

A QR code traceability system was embedded directly into the top case using dual extrusion printing.

The QR code linked directly to a digital manufacturing record PDF, creating a component-level digital twin system.

Figure 4 — Embedded QR code used for digital traceability.
Week 8

FESTO CP Lab Validation

Final iterations were tested on the FESTO CP Lab automated assembly line. The measuring station validated dimensional accuracy, while the muscle press station verified snap-fit assembly behaviour.

Figure 5 — FESTO CP Lab automated assembly validation.
Week 9

Final Recovery & Submission

Multiple physical iterations were lost before submission. Final parts were rebuilt using accumulated engineering knowledge and previously validated manufacturing parameters.

Figure 6 — Final validated phone case assembly.
