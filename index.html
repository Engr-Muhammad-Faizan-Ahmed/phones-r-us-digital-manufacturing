The Engineering Journey

Week 1 — PrintCity Induction

The journey started with the mandatory additive manufacturing induction at PrintCity led by Gary Buller. The session focused on machine operation, QR-based machine access, slicing workflow, and the importance of design responsibility in additive manufacturing.

First Prototype Print

The first bottom case was printed in black PLA. Although geometrically successful, the dark material made surface inspection and dimensional analysis difficult, leading to the transition toward white PLA material.

Fusion 360 CAD Development

The bottom case was rapidly completed following the provided tutorial workflow, while the top case required interpreting engineering drawings independently and redesigning geometry through multiple iterations.

Daily PrintCity Experimentation

From Week 4 onward, continuous experimentation became routine. Multiple prints were prepared every morning before lectures, testing different tolerances, support structures, print speeds, surface quality settings, and snap-fit dimensions.

QR Code Integration Challenge

One major challenge involved embedding a scannable QR code within limited geometry dimensions while maintaining both aesthetics and functionality. Dual-material printing using white PLA and black filament was tested to maximise contrast and readability.

FESTO CP Lab Validation

During Week 8, multiple iterations were tested on the FESTO CP Lab. Some parts passed the Measuring station but failed during the Muscle Press operation due to excessive snap-fit force or dimensional inaccuracies.

Final Recovery Sprint

Two days before submission, several bags containing printed parts were accidentally left on a bus. The remaining time was used to rapidly reproduce improved versions using accumulated manufacturing knowledge and validated tolerances.

Final Successful Assembly

The final iterations successfully passed the FESTO measuring and muscle press validation stages while achieving the intended snap-fit behaviour and assembly performance.
